How It Works

Technology

Geometry First. Always.

ODEN's breakthroughs come from mechanical architecture, not electronics. By embedding intelligence into the physical structure itself, we eliminate components, reduce failure modes, and unlock capabilities no existing prosthetic can match.

01

Independent Finger Splay

Each finger moves laterally as well as curling — just like a real hand. A novel joint architecture makes this possible without the complexity of traditional robotic approaches.

02

Passive Adaptive Grip

The hand conforms to objects through physics, not software. A proprietary compliance system means each finger naturally adapts to what it touches — no sensors, no latency, no failure modes.

03

Grows With You

Start simple, grow capable. The hand begins with intuitive control and progressively unlocks more dexterity as you learn. You're always in charge — the system adapts to you, not the other way around.

Over 25 million people worldwide live with upper limb loss. Existing prosthetics force a choice between cosmetic appearance and basic grip function. None offer the lateral finger movement fundamental to how humans actually use their hands. ODEN closes that gap.

Restoring what was lost. Not approximating it.

The Company

Aryx Industries

Aryx Industries is building the biomimetic hardware platform for human motion — starting with the hand. ODEN is the first product in a roadmap that begins with restoring natural hand function for upper limb amputees and extends into broader rehabilitation and assistive technologies.

Our approach centres on geometry-first mechanical design: solving problems through physical architecture rather than computational complexity. The result is hardware that is simpler, more reliable, and fundamentally more capable than existing approaches.

Founded by Maya Marnold, an engineer spanning mechanical design, embedded systems, and intelligent control. European designed. European engineered. European manufactured.
